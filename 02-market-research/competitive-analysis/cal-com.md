# Cal.com - Competitive Analysis

## Company Overview
- **Founded:** 2021 (originally Calendso)
- **Headquarters:** San Francisco, California, USA
- **Company Size:** 50-100 employees
- **Funding:** ~$32M raised (Series A in 2022)
- **Target Market:** Developers, tech-savvy users, privacy-conscious organizations, enterprises wanting self-hosted solutions

## Product Description
Cal.com is an open-source scheduling infrastructure platform. It positions itself as the "open-source Calendly alternative" with full self-hosting capabilities and extensive customization options.

## Strengths
- **Open source:** Full transparency, community contributions, no vendor lock-in
- **Self-hosting option:** Complete data control and privacy compliance
- **Highly customizable:** White-label, custom workflows, extensive API
- **Developer-friendly:** Well-documented API, webhooks, and integrations
- **Competitive pricing:** More affordable than Calendly at scale
- **Modern tech stack:** Built with Next.js, responsive and fast

## Weaknesses
- **Younger product:** Less mature than established competitors
- **Smaller ecosystem:** Fewer native integrations than Calendly
- **Self-hosting complexity:** Requires technical expertise to deploy and maintain
- **Brand recognition:** Less known outside developer communities
- **Support limitations:** Community support for open-source; paid support requires higher tiers
- **Feature parity gaps:** Some advanced features still in development

## Pricing (as of 2024)
- **Free:** Open source, self-hosted (unlimited)
- **Individual:** $0/month - basic cloud hosting
- **Team:** $15/user/month - team features
- **Organization:** $37/user/month - advanced admin, SSO
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing

## Key Differentiators
- Only major open-source alternative in the space
- Self-hosting enables GDPR/HIPAA compliance
- Appeals strongly to developer and privacy-focused audiences
