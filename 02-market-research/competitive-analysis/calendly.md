# Calendly - Competitive Analysis

## Company Overview
- **Founded:** 2013 by Tope Awotona
- **Headquarters:** Atlanta, Georgia, USA
- **Company Size:** 500+ employees
- **Funding:** Over $350M raised; valued at $3B+ (2021)
- **Target Market:** Professionals, sales teams, recruiters, small businesses

## Product Description
Calendly is a scheduling automation platform that eliminates the back-and-forth of finding meeting times. Users share their availability via a link, and invitees can book available slots directly.

## Strengths
- **Ease of use:** Extremely simple onboarding and intuitive interface
- **Strong brand recognition:** Market leader in scheduling space
- **Extensive integrations:** Works with Google, Outlook, Zoom, Salesforce, HubSpot, and 100+ tools
- **Freemium model:** Generous free tier attracts individual users
- **Team scheduling:** Robust features for round-robin, collective, and pooled availability
- **Reliability:** High uptime and trusted by enterprise customers

## Weaknesses
- **Limited customization:** Branding options restricted on lower tiers
- **Pricing at scale:** Can become expensive for larger teams
- **Feature bloat:** Has grown beyond "lightweight" for some users
- **No built-in calendar:** Requires integration with existing calendar apps
- **Limited offline functionality:** Fully cloud-dependent

## Pricing (as of 2024)
- **Free:** Basic scheduling, 1 event type
- **Standard:** $10/user/month - unlimited event types, integrations
- **Teams:** $16/user/month - team features, analytics
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing

## Key Differentiators
- First-mover advantage in modern scheduling tools
- Strong enterprise adoption
- Extensive API and workflow automation capabilities
