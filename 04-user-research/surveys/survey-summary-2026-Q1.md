# Survey Summary: 2026 Q1

**Survey period:** January - March 2026
**Respondents:** 147 (89 freelancers, 58 team leads/managers)

---

## 1. Tool Usage & Pain Points

**Current tool landscape:**
- 73% use 4+ tools daily for project/task management
- Most common combination: Slack + spreadsheets + domain-specific tool (Figma, GitHub, etc.)
- Only 12% feel they have a "single source of truth" for project status

**Top frustrations (ranked):**
- Context switching between tools (68%)
- Manual status updates and reporting (54%)
- Information getting lost between platforms (51%)
- Time spent on admin vs. actual work (47%)

**Tool adoption barriers:**
- "Too much effort to migrate" (61%)
- "Team won't use it consistently" (44%)
- "Doesn't integrate with existing tools" (39%)

---

## 2. Communication & Visibility

**Freelancer-specific:**
- 78% spend 3+ hours/week on client status communication
- 62% have lost a client or had conflict due to "communication gaps"
- 41% maintain separate internal vs. client-facing project views

**Team lead-specific:**
- 81% say they lack real-time visibility into team capacity
- 67% rely on meetings (not tools) to understand project status
- 59% report maintaining multiple versions of project updates for different audiences

**Shared findings:**
- 84% prefer async updates over scheduled check-ins
- 69% want "passive visibility" - showing progress without active reporting

---

## 3. Growth & Scaling Challenges

**Freelancers on scaling:**
- 52% turned down work in the past 6 months due to capacity uncertainty
- 34% considered hiring help but didn't due to "coordination overhead"
- Only 19% track client profitability in any structured way

**Team leads on process:**
- 71% say process standardization attempts have failed at least once
- Top reason for failure: "Adds friction without clear benefit" (58%)
- 46% say onboarding new team members takes longer than it should due to poor documentation

**Willingness to pay:**
- Freelancers: median $25/month for tool that saves 2+ hours/week
- Team leads: median $15/user/month for tool that reduces meeting time by 25%
